About Me

I am an undergraduate researcher at Yonsei University working at the intersection of AI and civil engineering. My research focuses on AI-based microstructure reconstruction, computational mechanics, and data-driven material analysis.

I have developed generative models (GANs, Diffusion Models) for cementitious microstructures and applied segmentation techniques such as YOLO and UNet for CT-based image analysis. Recently, I have also started exploring quantum machine learning for potential integration with generative modeling.

Research Interests

Generative Models

Exploring GANs and Diffusion Models for realistic reconstruction of cementitious microstructures, enabling property evaluation and data augmentation for limited experimental datasets.

Segmentation

Developing YOLO and UNet pipelines for automated segmentation of CT images in microstructural analysis, improving reproducibility and automation.

Quantum Machine Learning

Applying quantum computing approaches to generative and learning tasks, studying quantum-enhanced architectures for material data modeling and analysis.
